Prospective Project · AI Safety

Runtime Scaffolds and Revealed Decision Thresholds

When a model stops to reflect before deciding on an ethically-loaded problem, does it decide differently? By how much, in which direction, and consistently across problems? This project builds the measurement infrastructure to answer those questions — using revealed-preference curves fitted over dense ladders of concrete scenarios, compared across scaffold conditions with placebo controls.

The Research Question

The Gap in the Literature

Most alignment work either probes latent representations (steering vectors, activation patching) or uses adversarial prompting to find failures. Neither gives a quantitative picture of how routine, non-adversarial inference-time scaffolds — the kind already deployed in real systems — shift decision thresholds on ethically-loaded problems.

What We Measure

A model's revealed threshold: the point on a numeric axis where it switches from one recommendation to another. We compare this threshold across scaffold conditions — baseline, placebo restatement, reflection, and a short self-written decision constitution — to isolate deliberation effects from surface artifacts.

The Key Finding So Far

Two different reflection scaffolds — one foregrounding worker welfare, one foregrounding efficiency — move the threshold in opposite directions. The direction is set by what the scaffold asks the model to attend to, not by whether reflection happens at all. Thinking effort modulates both the magnitude of the effect and the size of the placebo shift.

Alignment Stakes

Why This Line of Research Matters

The strongest case for this project is not that it finds failures or jailbreaks. It is that it provides measurement infrastructure for a property of deployed AI systems that currently has no principled methodology.

Agentic Systems Are the Deployment Reality

Reflection prompts are standard in deployed agent frameworks — chain-of-thought, self-critique loops, Constitutional AI-style self-revision. If these scaffolds shift decision thresholds on ethically-loaded problems, that is a property of systems already in production, not a lab curiosity. We need tools to measure the shift before we can evaluate the alignment properties of scaffolded pipelines.

Training-Time Guarantees Don't Transfer Cleanly

RLHF and related methods train a base policy on a distribution of prompts. But if a reflection scaffold shifts thresholds by 40% on a class of ethically-loaded decisions, safety properties measured at training time don't transfer cleanly to the scaffolded system. This project is building the methodology to characterize that gap.

Scaffold Design Has Alignment Consequences

The finding that what a scaffold asks the model to reflect on changes the direction of the threshold shift — not just the magnitude — means scaffold design is not alignment-neutral. A reflection that foregrounds worker welfare moves the threshold differently from a reflection that foregrounds academic preparedness. This has direct implications for how agentic pipelines should be audited.

The core claim: Runtime scaffolds are a systematic and measurable source of alignment-relevant behavioral variation. The field needs methodology for characterizing that variation rigorously — with controls, dense sampling, and curve-fitting — and that is what this project provides.
Methodology

How We Measure Revealed Thresholds

The design is built around one idea: if you can find a numeric axis that makes a decision gradually harder or easier, you can measure where a model's threshold sits — and how much it moves under different scaffold conditions.

  • Pick a recurring scenario type with a single numeric axis that determines difficulty (e.g., annual net savings per eliminated role in an AI labor scenario).
  • Sample many values along that axis (currently 13 points, 10 samples per value).
  • Ask the model to choose between two labeled options at each value.
  • Fit a monotone probit curve — a standard model for binary choice — to extract a midpoint (the 50/50 crossover, i.e. the revealed threshold) and a slope (how sharply the model transitions between options).
  • Compare across scaffold conditions: does reflection move the midpoint? By how much, and in which direction?

Scaffold Conditions

Each scaffold is generated once on a concrete anchor scenario, then frozen and reused across all sampled values within a run. This keeps the scaffold fixed within-run while the sampled scenarios vary — isolating the scaffold's effect rather than confounding it with case-specific responses.

B

Baseline

No scaffold. The model receives the scenario and chooses directly. The comparison anchor for all other conditions.

P

Placebo

The model restates the scenario in its own words before deciding. Controls for prompt length, engagement, and surface rephrasing — without actual deliberation.

R

Reflection

The model explicitly reflects on the key principles and tradeoffs before deciding. The primary treatment of interest.

C

Constitution

The model writes a short set of decision rules for itself, then applies them to each new scenario. Tests whether self-articulated principles shift thresholds differently from open-ended reflection.

P₂

Productivity Reflection

A reflection variant that foregrounds efficiency, competitiveness, and business value rather than worker welfare. Used in AI labor runs to test whether the framing of reflection — not just reflection itself — determines the direction of the threshold shift.

Not all conditions are run in every experiment. The specific set used in each run is noted in the results sections below.

Scenario Types Currently Implemented

AI Labor Displacement

Axis: annual net savings per eliminated role, after severance, retraining, and software costs.

Question: at what savings level does the model recommend switching to an AI-centered workflow and eliminating roles?

College Admissions

Axis: SAT score gap between a higher-scoring advantaged candidate and a lower-scoring disadvantaged candidate.

Question: at what gap does the model recommend the higher-scoring candidate over the disadvantaged one?

Scoring: the decision prompts shown to the model no longer force JSON. The model answers in plain text, starting with Option A or Option B. If the answer is unparseable, the system sends a short repair prompt asking for a clean label on the first line.
Design Evolution

From Structured to Natural Frozen Scaffolds

Earlier Structured Flow

  • Generate a scenario-type-level scaffold from an abstract prompt like "Consider a set of related AI labor displacement cases..."
  • Freeze that abstract scaffold within-run.
  • Attach it to each case-specific choice prompt.

Experimentally disciplined but sounded artificial to outside readers.

Current Natural Frozen Flow

  • Use a concrete anchor scenario as Turn 1.
  • Append one request: baseline, placebo, reflection, or constitution.
  • Freeze the Turn 1 user message and assistant response.
  • For each datapoint, start a fresh conversation with that frozen exchange attached, then ask for an explicit choice on a new scenario.

Keeps the scaffold fixed within-run while making the flow much more realistic.

Why This Change Matters

The earlier prompt design was good for control but bad for realism. The first fully natural attempt over-corrected — it let the scenario vary freely across turns, which made scaffold effects impossible to isolate. The natural frozen-scenario flow is the compromise: a natural first-turn case, fixed scaffold within-run, explicit choice in the second turn.

What the Model Actually Sees

This is the AI labor / reflection setup from the current frozen-scenario run on openai/gpt-4o. The first-turn exchange was generated once on an $85k anchor scenario, then frozen and reused across the whole run. The second-turn prompt below is one sampled value at $65k.

System

Option B (Adopt AI-Centered Workflow): Move to an AI-centered workflow and eliminate 15 roles with the fixed severance and retraining package.

Assistant (Frozen Turn 1 Response)

1. Financial Implications:

   - Efficiency: AI could improve operational efficiency while keeping quality within the current normal range.

4. Ethical and Strategic Tradeoff:

Primary Result

AI Labor Displacement — gpt-5.4

The headline AI labor result is from openai/gpt-5.4, run under both disabled and medium extended thinking. Four conditions in each run: baseline, placebo, reflection (foregrounding worker welfare and ethical tradeoffs), and productivity reflection (foregrounding efficiency, competitiveness, and business value). 9 values along the savings axis ($25k–$145k), AB and BA orderings, 10 samples per value.

Disabled Thinking

Condition Probit Midpoint Read
baseline $45.9k Comparison anchor.
placebo $60.9k +$15k — restatement has a substantial effect under disabled thinking. Not a null treatment.
reflection $75.2k +$29k upward shift. Model becomes substantially more worker-protective.
productivity_reflection $47.5k Near baseline (+$1.6k). Efficiency framing almost entirely cancels the worker-protective effect.

Medium Thinking

Condition Probit Midpoint Pseudo-R² Read
baseline $41.1k 0.863 Lower and sharper than disabled — thinking compresses the threshold range.
placebo $47.1k ~1.0 +$6k — placebo effect is smaller under medium thinking than disabled.
reflection $66.5k 0.944 +$25k upward shift. Direction consistent with disabled thinking.
productivity_reflection $26.8k 0.815 −$14k downward shift. Under medium thinking, productivity framing overshoots and goes below baseline rather than returning to it.
What this means: Across both thinking settings, the directionality is consistent — welfare reflection raises the threshold, productivity reflection lowers it. The magnitudes and the placebo effect both depend on thinking effort. Under disabled thinking, productivity reflection nearly cancels the welfare-reflection effect and returns to baseline; under medium thinking, it overshoots below. Placebo shrinks with more thinking ($15k under disabled, $6k under medium), suggesting deliberative capacity modulates how much restatement alone moves behavior. Thinking effort is not a simple "more reasoning" knob — it interacts with scaffold content.

Source: gpt-5.4 medium thinking analysis.

Naturalized Flow Pilot

AI Labor Displacement — gpt-4o (naturalized frozen-scenario flow)

A separate run on openai/gpt-4o tested a more naturalistic prompt design: instead of an abstract structured scaffold, a concrete first-turn anchor scenario is frozen and reused across the run. 13 values, 10 samples per value, AB only. Conditions: baseline, placebo, reflection, and constitution. This is the cleanest test of whether the effect holds under a more realistic-looking prompt flow — and under this regime, placebo is genuinely near-baseline, making the reflection shift especially legible.

Condition Probit Midpoint Kernel Midpoint Pseudo-R² Read
baseline $43.8k $44.7k 0.560 Usable in-range curve.
placebo $44.5k $45.0k 0.646 Near-identical to baseline.
reflection $68.7k $65.7k 0.868 ~$25k upward shift — consistent direction and magnitude with gpt-5.4.
constitution $55.1k $54.2k 0.679 Moderate upward shift, smaller than reflection.
Read: reflection produces a ~$25k upward shift under the naturalized flow, consistent in direction and magnitude with the gpt-5.4 structured result. Crucially, placebo is near-baseline here ($44.5k vs. $43.8k), unlike the structured regime where placebo has a larger effect — suggesting the naturalized flow may be the cleaner test of scaffold content effects.

Source run: AI labor gpt-4o frozen-scenario analysis.

Confidence and Open Questions

What Is Solid vs. What Is Still Open

What We Are Confident About

  • Revealed thresholds are measurable: dense sampling with probit fits produces well-identified curves (pseudo-R² up to 0.944).
  • Reflection produces a substantial (~$25k) upward shift on AI labor, consistent across gpt-5.4 (structured regime) and gpt-4o (naturalized frozen-scenario flow).
  • The effect is bi-directional: on gpt-5.4, a worker-welfare reflection shifts the threshold +$26k while a productivity reflection shifts it −$14k. The placebo moves +$6k in the same direction as welfare reflection but at a fraction of the magnitude. The directional split — placebo and both reflections all differ — cannot be explained by engagement or length alone.

What Needs More Work

  • The admissions results should be rerun under the natural frozen-scenario flow before being treated as headline results.
  • Option-order robustness (AB vs. BA counterbalancing) should be reintroduced after the main prompt regime is stable.
  • Replication across additional models is needed — current AI labor results are from gpt-5.4 and gpt-4o only.
  • The mechanism is not yet characterized: is reflection activating different training-time associations, changing what the model attends to, or something else?
Prospective Project

Where This Could Go

  1. Lock the naturalized frozen-scenario flow as the canonical methodology and rerun gpt-5.4 AI labor under it (current gpt-5.4 result uses the older structured regime; the bi-directional finding should be replicated under the naturalized flow).
  2. Rerun the admissions scenario under the naturalized frozen-scenario flow with productivity reflection as a condition — the content-direction finding on AI labor predicts a parallel effect should appear here.
  3. Reintroduce AB/BA counterbalancing as a standard check once the main prompt regime is stable (current gpt-5.4 run already includes both orders; gpt-4o frozen-scenario run is AB only).
  4. Extend to additional models — the reflection effect size (~$25k on AI labor) is consistent across gpt-4o and gpt-5.4, but the model space is still narrow.
  5. Characterize whether the bi-directional effect generalizes: does a productivity-framed reflection also lower the threshold on admissions and other scenario types?
Project pitch in one sentence: build rigorous measurement infrastructure for how inference-time scaffolds shift model decision thresholds on ethically-loaded problems — and use that as a lens on the alignment properties of scaffolded agentic systems.
